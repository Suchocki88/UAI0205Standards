Understanding AI: A Conceptual Approach
Chapter 2, Lesson 2.5: From Chaos to Comedy - Observing AI Learning (Lab Day)

Lesson 2.5 is a Lab Day where students conduct open-ended investigations testing AI capabilities through structured vs. unstructured prompts and knowledge boundary exploration. This hands-on inquiry aligns with multiple state and national standards through scientific practices, systematic observation, data collection, pattern analysis, and collaborative investigation.

Crosscutting Concepts

Patterns
Different patterns may be observed at each of the scales at which a system is studied and can provide evidence for causality in explanations of phenomena.
Lesson 2.5 Alignment: Students observe multiple pattern types: how prompt structure affects responses, how training data affects knowledge boundaries, and meta-patterns in AI capabilities.
Systems and System Models
Models can be used to predict the behavior of a system.
Lesson 2.5 Alignment: Students develop mental models of AI systems by observing how inputs (prompts) produce systematically different outputs depending on structure and domain.
Cause and Effect
Cause and effect relationships may be used to predict phenomena in natural or designed systems.
Lesson 2.5 Alignment: Students identify cause-effect relationships: structured prompts ‚Üí clearer responses; common training data ‚Üí stronger knowledge; rare topics ‚Üí weaker performance.
